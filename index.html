Q1. What is a solar system?

Ans: A solar system is made up of a star (like our Sun) at the center, with planets, moons, asteroids, and comets orbiting around it, all held together by the star's gravitational pull.

Q2. What is a galaxy?

Ans: A galaxy is a vast collection of billions of stars, along with their solar systems, gas, and dust, all bound together by gravity, appearing as a large, sprawling structure in space.

Q3. Where is our solar system located?

Ans: Our solar system is located within the Milky Way galaxy.

Q4. How does a solar system form?

Ans: A solar system is thought to form from a collapsing cloud of gas and dust, with the densest part becoming the star and the remaining material forming planets and other objects.

Q5. Why does the Moon change its shape daily?

Ans: The Moon changes its shape daily because of its phases, which result from its orbit around Earth. As it moves, different portions of its sunlit side become visible from Earth, creating the appearance of changing shapes, from new moon to full moon and back.

Q6. Meteors are not visible during the day?

Ans: Meteors are not visible during the day because sunlight is too bright, overpowering the faint light emitted by meteors as they burn up in Earth's atmosphere.

Q7. Explain why we see the same side of the moon?

Ans: We see the same side of the Moon because it is tidally locked to Earth. This means the Moon's rotation period matches its orbit around Earth, keeping one side always facing us.

Q8. Do stars emit light only during the night?

Ans: No, stars emit light all the time, but we see them at night because the Sun's brightness during the day outshines their faint light.

Q9. Why do stars twinkle?

Ans: Stars twinkle because their light passes through Earth's atmosphere, where it is bent and scattered by varying air densities, causing the light to appear to flicker.

Q10. How huge is the planetary group?

Ans: The planetary group, or the solar system, is vast. It spans about 100,000 astronomical units (AU), with 1 AU being the distance between Earth and the Sun (about 150 million kilometers). This includes the Sun, planets, moons, asteroids, comets, and the farthest edge of the Oort Cloud.

Q11. What are the different types of stars?

Ans: The different types of stars include:
1. Main sequence stars – Like the Sun, they fuse hydrogen into helium.
2. Red giants – Large, cool stars that have expanded after using up their hydrogen.
3. White dwarfs – Dense, leftover cores of stars that have exhausted their fuel.
4. Neutron stars – Extremely dense remnants of supernova explosions.
5. Supergiants – Very large, bright stars, often nearing the end of their life cycle.
6. Black holes – Not stars, but the result of massive stars collapsing under gravity.

Q16. What is the difference between a star and a planet?

Ans: A star is a massive, hot ball of gas that generates its own light, while a planet is a celestial body that orbits a star and does not generate its own light.
